Start iron supplementation at 2 weeks for premature infants and continue for 12-15 months.

If not getting enough breastmilk, increase frequency of feeds to 10 times in 24 hrs and pump after breastfeeding, giving any extracted milk to the baby at the next feed.

If mother does not want to give vitamin D to baby (recommended), an alternative is for the mother to take 6000 IU daily of Vit D while breastfeeding. I don't think this technique works for iron.

Vegan mothers who don't use B12 supplements should give Vit B12 supplements to baby.

References

  • Uptodate. Patient Education: Breastfeeding Guide (Beyond the Basics). Appropriate number of wet diapers per day. Appropriate feeding frequency. Using vitamin D in breastfeeding mother instead of baby.
  • Uptodate. . Expected growth of infants.
  • AAFP. Care of the Premature Infant: Part I. Monitoring Growth and Development. States that iron should be started at 2 weeks to 1 month for premature infants.
