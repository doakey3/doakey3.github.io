Choosing a Combined Oral Contraceptive

Guidelines: Where possible, use a monophasic pill with the 24/4 pattern (24 treatment days, 4 placebo).

Aura: sensory disturbance that may include flashes of light, blind spots, and tingling in hands or face that precede a migraine headache.

(Note, for this table, the full list of treatment options is huge. I just focused on what I would try first in a patient.)

Problem Recommended Medication
Polycystic Ovarian Syndrome norethindrone-ethinyl estradiol 1 mg-20 mcg oral tablet daily
Abnormal uterine bleeding Mirena is the best (71 to 95% reduction in bleeding). oral Medroxyprogesterone (Provera) can reduce bleeding by 87%, but the oral form does not prevent pregnancy. Medroxyprogesterone can be used off-label to treat acute vaginal bleeding (20 mg PO TID x 7 days). A combined oral contraceptive will only reduce bleeding by 35-69% and there is only 1 FDA approved med and it is not in the military formulary. (Natazia)

If a pre-menopausal woman wants a pill for AUB, start medoxyprogesterone 5 mg by mouth daily x 10 days starting at day 16 of the menstrual cycle every cycle.
Menstrual Migraines Use an estrogen-progestin pill with 10-30 mcg Estrogen (Ex: Gianvi or Yaz)
Premenstrual syndrome/premenstrual dysphoric disorder drospirenone-ethinyl estradiol 3 mg-0.02 mg oral tablet daily. If ineffective after 3 months, increase to 3 mg-0.03 mg daily
Acne (Low androgen) Norgestimate-Ethinyl estradiol 0.25 mg-35 mcg 1 tablet daily
Fibrocystic breast Norethindrone-Ethinyl estradiol 1 mg-20mcg 1 tablet daily
Dysmenorrhea Norethindrone-Ethinyl estradiol 1 mg-20mcg 1 tablet daily
