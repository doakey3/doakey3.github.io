Lower BP w/o Meds

InterventionDescriptionExpected BP Reduction
Salt Reduction The American Heart Association set 1500 mg/day of sodium as the recommended upper limit of intake for all Americans.
Systolic:  1.9 - 4.8
Diastolic:  1.1 - 2.5
Weight loss Weight loss in overweight or obese individuals can lead to a significant fall in blood pressure independent of exercise. 0.5 - 2 for every 1 kg of weight lost
DASH diet A diet high in vegetables, fruits, low-fat dairy products, whole grains, poultry, fish, and nuts and low in sweets, sugar-sweetened beverages, and red meats.
Systolic:  6
Diastolic:  4
Exercise At least 3 sessions of moderate-intensity aerobic exercise lasting approximately 40 minutes for a period of 12 weeks has been shown to reduce blood pressure independent of weight loss.
Systolic:  4-6
Diastolic:  3
Limited alcohol intake Women who consume two or more alcoholic beverages per day and men who have three or more drinks per day have a significantly increased incidence of hypertension compared with nondrinkers.
