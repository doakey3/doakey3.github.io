Shoulder Pain Template



Job's Test

Pain with this movement can suggests weakness/tear of the supraspinatus.

External Rotation

Patient externally rotates against resistance. Pain with this movement can suggests weakness/tear of the infraspinatus or the teres minor.

Lift-off

Pain with this movement suggests weakness/tear of the subscapularis.

Belly Press

Pain with this movement suggests weakness/tear of the subscapularis.

Neer's

Reproduction of pain at maximum extension of the shoulder indicates impingement of the rotator cuff tendons under the coracoacromial arch.

Hawkin's

Reproduction of pain suggests subacromial impingement or rotator cuff tendonitis.

Drop Arm

Passively abduct arm, then instruct patient to slowly lower it. If they cannot smoothly lower the arm all the way to the waist, it signifies a rotator cuff tear or supraspinatus dysfunction.

Cross-arm

Prevent movement of the scapula while adducting the arm flexed 90 degrees at the elbow. Pain at the acromioclavicular joint indicates dysfunction at this location (such as an AC separation).

Sulcus Sign

Downward humeral traction. A depression forming around the acromion indicates glenohumeral instability.

Speeds

Flex shoulder and extend elbow so arm is horizontal with floor. Downward pressure is applied to the supinated forearm. Pain in the bicipital groove indicates bicipital tendonopathy.

Yergason's

Elbow flexed 90 degrees and hand supinated so that thumb is vertical. The examiner resists further supination and elbow flexion. Pain in the bicipital groove indicates bicipital tendinosis. Popping in and out of the groove indicates a tear of the transverse humeral ligament.

Spurling's

Cervical spine extended, head rotated toward the affected shoulder, axial load applied. Reproduction of pain indicates compression of a cervical nerve root ganglion.

Apprehension Test

Patient is supine. The examiner externally rotates the shoulder. If the patient shows apprehension, it indicates glenohumeral joint instability.
