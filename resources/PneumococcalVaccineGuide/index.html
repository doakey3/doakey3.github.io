Pneumococcal Vaccination Guide

PCV13 then PPSV23

Give the PSCV13 vaccine followed by PPSV23 after 8 or more weeks later to any patient aged 19-64 who have never received a pneumococcal vaccine and meet any of the following conditions:

  • Chronic kidney disease, especially if protein-losing nephropathy is present
  • HIV
  • Hematologic malignancy (leukemia, lymphoma, multiple myeloma)
  • Generalized malignancy (patients with solid tumors treated with chemotherapy and/or metastatic disease)
  • Solid organ transplant recipients
  • Hematopoetic stem cell transplant recipient
  • Iatrogenic immunosuppression (including long-term glucocorticoids)
  • Congenital immunosuppression (B or T lymphocyte deficiency, complement deficiency, phagocytic disorders)
  • Impaired splenic function
  • Cochlear implant
  • Cerebrospinal leak
  • History of pneumococal meningitis

PPSV23

PPSV23 should be given to patients aged 19-64 who have never received a pneumonia shot if they have any of the following conditions:

  • Chronic heart disease
  • Chronic lung disease
  • Chronic liver disease
  • Poorly controlled diabetes mellitus
  • Current cigarette smoking
  • Alcohol use disorder

PPSV23 Revaccination

  • Impaired splenic function, cerebrospinal fluid leak, cochlear implant, or any immunocompromising condition who has not received the PPSV23 vaccine in the last 5 years
  • Anyone else who met the requirement for vaccination before age 65 who has not received the vaccine in the last 10 years.
  • Any adult over the age of 65 and has not received the vaccine in the last 10 years.

NOTE: For patients who have been treated for an invasive pneumococcal disease, the dosing varies. See https://www.uptodate.com/contents/pneumococcal-vaccination-in-adults
