Well Child Visits by Age

Age Recommended Vaccines Routine Screenings
Newborn Hep B (if not done in nursery) Bilirubin level, EPDS for mother, Hearing
3-4 days Bilirubin level, EPDS for mother, Weight; use newborn note template
2 Weeks EPDS for mother, Metabolic screen (ensure 2nd is done); use the newborn template
2 Months Pediarix (Hep B, DTaP, IPV), PCV, Hib, Rotateq EPDS for mother
4 Months Pediarix (Hep B, DTaP, IPV), PCV, Hib, Rotateq Maternal Depression
6 Months Pediarix (Hep B, DTaP, IPV), PCV, Flu, consider iron supplement Maternal Depression, TB
9 Months Flu #2, if not yet given (Kids who've never had flu shots should get 2 flu vax this season, w/the 2nd given by end of Oct) ASQ
12 Months Hib, PCV, MMR, Varicella, Hep A Hct/Hgb lab test, Dental, Lead survey or blood test if any suspicion, TB, Vision
15 Months DTaP Dental, Lead survey (if not done at 12 mo), Vision (if not done at 12 mo)
18 Months Hep A #2 (6 months after #1) ASQ, MCHAT, Dental, TB, Vision (if not done at 12 mo), Lead survey (if not done at 12 mo)
24 Months Flu ASQ, MCHAT, Dental, Lead survey, TB, Vision,
3 Years Flu BP, Dental, Lead survey, TB, Vision
4 Years DTaP, IPV, MMR, Varicella, Flu BP, Dental, Lead survey, TB, Vision
5-6 Years Flu BP, Dental, Lead survey, TB, Vision
7-10 Years Flu BP, TB, Vision, Lipid 9-11
11 Years Tdap, Menactra, HPV #1, Flu annually BP, Teen Screen/HEADSSS, PHQ-2-Columbia, STI labs (+/-), Vision, Lipid panel 9-11
12-14 Years HPV #2 if not yet given (6 months after #1), Flu BP, Teen Screen/HEADSSS, PHQ-2-Columbia, STI labs (+/-), Vision
15 Years 3 doses HPV (timing = 0, 1, 6 months) if not yet given or finish the 2-dose series if first dose was given before age 15 (6-12 months after the first dose), Flu BP, Teen Screen/HEADSSS, PHQ-2-Columbia, STI labs (+/-), Vision, Hep C
16 Years Menactra, Flu BP, Teen Screen/HEADSSS, PHQ-2-Columbia, STI labs (+/-), Vision, Hep C (if not already done)
17 Years BP, Teen Screen/HEADSSS, Lipid (once 17-21 yo), Hep C (if not already done), PHQ-2-Columbia, STI labs (+/-), Vision

NOTES: Height and weight should be assessed at every well-visit. Head circumference should be assessed through 24 months. Weight/length should be assessed for age < 24 months, BMI should be assessed for age > 24 months. Assess development at every well visit. Standardized developmental screening tools are recommended at 9, 18, and 30 months. Use a handheld vision screener until 4 years old.

Abbreviations

ASQ: Ages and Stages questionnaire BP: Blood pressure DTaP: Diptheria, tetanus, pertussis Flu: Influenza vaccine Hct/Hgb: Hematocrit and hemoglobin HEADSSS: Teen risk assessment and counseling without parent or guardian present Hep B: Hepatitis B vaccine Hep A: Hepatitis A vaccine Hib Haemophilus influenzae type b: a bacteria that causes meningitis, epiglottitis, and pneumonia. HPV: Human paillomavirus vaccine: causes genital warts and cervical cancer. IPV: Inactivated polio vaccine Lead: Lead poisoning questionnaire Lipid: Non-fasting lipid panel MCHAT: Modified checklist for autism in toddlers Menactra: Protects against the 4 most common types of meningococcal bacteria MMR: measles, mumps, rubella vaccine PCV: Pneumococcal vaccine RotaTeq: A vaccine for prevention of rotavirus gastroenteritis in infants. STI: Sexually transmitted infections TB: Tuberculosis screening questionnaire
