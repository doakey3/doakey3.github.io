Anterior Drawer

Patient hips flexed 45 degrees. Examiner sits on patients foot, tugs on upper tibia.

Positive if lack of stable end point or excessive anterior translation compared to contralateral side.

Positive test indicates ACL injury

Posterior Drawer

Patient hips flexed 45 degrees. Examiner sits on patients foot, pushes on upper tibia.

Positive if lack of stable end point or excessive posterior translation compared to contralateral side.

Positive test indicates PCL injury

Thessaly's Test

Patient stands on slightly flexed leg and rotates femur on tibia.

Positive if medial or lateral joint line discomfort or a sense of locking/ catching in the knee.

Positive test indicates injury to meniscus.

Varus Stress

Physician pulls medially on lower leg while pushing laterally on inner thigh.

Positive if pain along the lateral collateral ligament.

Positive test indicates lateral collateral ligament injury.
